INTRO

It is not as long a climb as Mont Ventoux nor as high or historic as Col du Tourmalet, but Alpe d'Huez has developed its own folklore and become the iconic Tour de France mountain to conquer. As riders pass each of its famous 21 bends they see the names of winners gone before, and around half a million frenetic fans create what the former white jersey winner, Andy Hampsten, described as "more cycling stadium than beautiful road". We take a closer look at the famous 14km route from Bourg d'Oisans to the Alpine resort above.
